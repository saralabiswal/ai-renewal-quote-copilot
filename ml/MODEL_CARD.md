# Standalone ML Model Card

## Model Bundle
- Active renewal risk: selected in `ml/model-registry.json`
- Active expansion propensity: selected in `ml/model-registry.json`
- Baseline candidates: `renewal_risk_sklearn`, `expansion_propensity_sklearn`
- Challenger candidates: `renewal_risk_xgboost`, `expansion_propensity_xgboost`
- Version: `v0.1-synthetic`
- Owner: RevOps Decision Intelligence
- Runtime: local Python, scikit-learn/XGBoost, joblib artifacts
- Feature schema: `renewal-features-v1`

## Intended Use
The model bundle is the default local decision-support layer for renewal recommendations.

The app defaults to ML-Assisted Rules:
- `RULES_ONLY`: deterministic rules only.
- `ML_SHADOW`: ML scores are captured for audit and comparison but do not change recommendations.
- `HYBRID_RULES_ML`: shown as ML-Assisted Rules in the UI; ML risk scores are blended into risk scoring while deterministic pricing guardrails remain final.

The bundle is intended to demonstrate a standalone, auditable AI/ML architecture for renewal decisioning.

## Not Intended Use
This model is not intended for autonomous pricing approval, contractual commitment, production revenue forecasting, or customer-facing explanation without human review.

## Training Data
Current standalone training data comes from `ml/data/synthetic-renewal-training.csv`.

The dataset is generated by `ml/synthetic_data.py` and contains 1,000 renewal line examples across 300 synthetic cases and 12 product archetypes.

The current labels are synthetic historical-like outcomes generated from the renewal data model:
- renewal risk label: observed risk score with rule, product, margin, escalation, and outcome noise
- expansion label: observed expansion acceptance
- supporting outcomes: renewal outcome, discount acceptance, reviewer override, ARR delta

This is stronger than training only on curated app seed rows because it gives the model broader product, scenario, commercial, and outcome variation. It is still not production generalization evidence.

Production training should use historical renewal outcomes, churn/contraction, accepted discount, quote approval override, expansion conversion, support history, usage trend, and post-renewal revenue realization.

## Features
The feature schema includes:
- usage and active-user adoption
- login trend
- support ticket and severity load
- CSAT
- current quantity, ARR, list price, net price, and discount
- account health and escalation count
- payment and adoption bands
- account segment and industry
- product family and charge model
- product archetype signals including margin band, strategic tier, attach type, and core-product flag
- rule baseline score and disposition

## Evaluation
Latest local evaluation report: `ml/reports/evaluation.json`.

The report includes:
- holdout row count
- 70/30 holdout protocol without evaluation leakage
- model selection across baseline and challenger candidates
- risk model MAE, RMSE, and R2
- expansion model accuracy, precision, recall, F1, and ROC AUC
- rule baseline comparisons
- artifact checksums
- label-source limitations

Because current labels are synthetic, strong metrics should be read as demo readiness and integration correctness, not as production predictive power.

## Explainability
Prediction responses include top contributing feature names from model feature importances for each line item.

Current explainability is intentionally lightweight. Production-grade explainability should add:
- per-prediction feature contribution values
- rule-vs-ML disagreement reason codes
- normalized business reason labels
- confidence bands
- shadow disagreement reports

## Guardrails
Pricing and approval guardrails remain deterministic. ML can adjust risk posture in ML-Assisted Rules mode, but floor price exceptions, approval requirements, and policy outcomes remain controlled by rules.

## Promotion Requirements
Before production promotion, require:
- historical labeled training data
- offline validation against holdout periods
- model card approval
- drift and missing-feature monitoring
- rollback version
- shadow-mode burn-in
- reviewer override analysis
- governance sign-off for hybrid activation
